Who's Who in the Feng Lab

A guide to Feng Lab members past and present.

Group Leader

Xiaoqi Feng

Xiaoqi studied at the University of Oxford for her PhD, under the supervision of Hugh Dickinson. After a postdoc at the University of California, Berkley, she opened her lab at the John Innes Centre in mid-2014 with a David Phillips Fellowship, and was awarded tenure in 2019. In 2018 she was named as one of the EMBO Young Investigators. She is grateful for funding and support from the John Innes Foundation, the BBSRC, the Gatsby Foundation and the ERC among others.

Postdocs

Jingyi Zhang

Jingyi completed her PhD at Shanghai Jiao Tong University. She is trying to understand the functions of the tapetum, which is the layer of somatic nurse cells around the male germline.



Shengbo He

Shengbo He also completed his PhD at Shanghai Jiao Tong University. He is fascinated by histone dynamics, especially of the linker histone H1. He has recently published a paper in eLife on H1 dynamics.

James Walker

James recently completed his PhD in the Feng lab, notably publishing as co-first author in Nature Genetics. He is interested in the evolution of DNA methylation mechanisms in land plants.

Martin Vickers

Martin is a bioinformatician, and has also been a computer scientist, sys-admin, and a geographer. His tools are instrumental to the lab's progress, and he has a forthcoming manuscript detailing new computational approaches for bisulfite sequencing.

Jincheng Long

Jincheng joined the Feng Lab in late 2017, after working on the chromatin remodeler DDM1 in maize. He is one of the most energetic members of the lab, and is working on germline DNA methylation.

Yalin Liu

Yalin's PhD was about de novo centromere formation in maize. She is now working on the heat-sensitivity of the male germline, as well as work on Marchantia.

Chris Morgan

Chris joins us from the Bomblies lab, and is an expert on meiosis and super-resolution microscopy.

Shaoli Zhou

The most recent arrival in the lab, Shaoli joins us fresh from perfecting single cell Hi-C in rice.

Students

Toby Buttress

Toby has been in the lab since his undergraduate days at UEA, and is now a fourth year PhD student. He is interested in the broad-scale chromatin dynamics over reproduction, and the mechanism of sperm chromatin compaction. He is an expert on the extended Star Wars universe.



Sam Deans

A former summer student and now PhD student in the lab, Sam is working on improving how we understand large DNA methylation datasets. His Star Wars knowledge is only Padawan level.
